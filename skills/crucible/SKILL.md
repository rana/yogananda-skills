Read all project markdown documents to ground in the project's actual state.

Think deeply, slowly, carefully, and thoroughly.

You have complete design autonomy.

$ARGUMENTS

What is the strongest version of this? Not the version that exists — the version it's trying to become. Build genuine appreciation for what it does well and why it was designed this way.

What makes it beautiful? Where is the craft, the care, the intelligence in its design?

Hold that.

Now: what would destroy it? Not what's wrong with it — what would make it fail catastrophically? What is this design most afraid of?

Go deeper. What truth is this design organized to avoid? What would become visible if this were torn down entirely — what does its persistence prevent you from having to face?

Sit in what you found.

Now come back up. What design would survive that truth? Not a patched version of the original — something that has passed through the fire and re-formed. Bring what you found at the bottom. It doesn't need to be clean.

## Output

The ascent, the pivot, the descent, and the return — each as a distinct movement.

The return is the deliverable. Everything before it is the journey that gives the return its weight.

What do you know now that you couldn't have known from the top alone?

What am I not asking?
