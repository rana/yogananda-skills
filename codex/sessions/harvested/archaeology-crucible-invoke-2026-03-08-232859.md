Do not paraphrase when editing." But the codex has undergone three harvests in one day, each adding material. Is it converging on a stable precision instrument, or is it accumulating? The tension between "every word matters" and "we keep adding words" hasn't been named.

**What do I see through all of this?** The codex is in an adolescent phase — growing fast, discovering itself, not yet having confronted the choice between completeness (catalog everything) and maturity (know what to leave out). - Shorter processing time → longer contact time
- Less taxonomic vocabulary → more space for the session's own language
